The Best Romance Anime to Watch, Sorted by What You're in the Mood For

Picking a romance anime from a flat top-10 list is a gamble, because “best” means nothing if you wanted a comedy and got handed a story that ends in tears. So instead of one ranking, this guide sorts the best romance anime by what you actually feel like watching tonight. Want to cry? There is a section for that. Want to laugh? That too. Every pick here is one fans return to, with a quick note on what makes it worth your time.

If you want to cry: the romance dramas

These are the ones people warn you about. Keep tissues nearby.

Your Lie in April follows a piano prodigy who stopped hearing music after his mother died, until a violinist drags him back to the keyboard. It is gorgeous, it is devastating, and the ending lands like a truck. Most “saddest anime” lists open with it for a reason.

A Silent Voice is a film about a former bully trying to make amends with the deaf girl he tormented as a kid. It handles guilt, redemption and self-worth with more honesty than most live-action dramas manage, and it never feels cheap about it.

Clannad (and especially its sequel, After Story) starts as a school comedy and slowly turns into one of the most quietly heartbreaking stories about family the medium has. The slow burn is the point.

Your Name is the gateway film that pulled millions into anime. Two teenagers swap bodies across time and distance, and the mystery of how they connect builds to a finale that earns every emotion. If you only watch one title from this whole guide, make it this one.

If you want to laugh: the rom-coms

Romance does not have to hurt. These keep things light while still making you root for the couple.

Kaguya-sama: Love is War turns a simple crush into open warfare, as two brilliant student council members each refuse to confess first. It is fast, sharp and genuinely funny, and the cast around the leads is just as strong.

Horimiya is the rare romance that skips the will-they-won’t-they entirely and lets the couple get together early, then explores what comes after. The result feels warm and real instead of frustrating.

My Dress-Up Darling pairs a shy doll craftsman with an outgoing gyaru who pulls him into cosplay. The chemistry is immediate, the tone is sweet, and it wraps a full arc into a single season.

Wotakoi: Love is Hard for Otaku is romance for grown-ups, following two adult gamers navigating a relationship between work and hobbies. If you are tired of high school settings, this is the palate cleanser.

If you want a school romance

The classic setting, done right. These are the ones that defined the subgenre.

Toradora is the blueprint. A scary-looking softie and a tiny, fierce classmate team up to help each other’s crushes and slowly fall for each other instead. The character growth is the best in the genre, and it remains the go-to recommendation for newcomers.

Fruits Basket (the 2019 remake) blends a found-family curse with a tender central romance, and its full three-season run gives every relationship room to breathe.

Kimi ni Todoke is a slow, gentle story about a misunderstood girl learning to make friends and fall in love. If you want something soft and low-stakes, this is it.

If you only have a couple of hours: the movies

No multi-season commitment required. These tell a complete love story in one sitting.

Beyond Your Name and A Silent Voice above, look at Weathering With You from the same director as Your Name, and the older classic 5 Centimeters per Second, a quietly melancholic film about distance pulling two people apart. For something more hopeful, I Want to Eat Your Pancreas delivers exactly the emotional wreckage the title promises.

Where to start if you are new to the genre

If this is your first romance anime, do not start with the hardest cry. Begin with Toradora or Horimiya for a series, since both are easy to follow and satisfying from start to finish. For a film, Your Name is the no-risk pick. Once those land, you will know whether you lean comedy or drama, and the sections above will point you to the next one.

Frequently asked questions

What is the number one romance anime?+

There is no single winner, but Your Lie in April and A Silent Voice are the two most recommended dramas, while Kaguya-sama: Love is War tops most rom-com lists. If you want the most universally loved film, Your Name is the safest pick.

Which romance anime is considered peak?+

Fans usually point to Your Lie in April, A Silent Voice, Clannad and Toradora as the genre's high points. For comedy, Kaguya-sama and Horimiya get the same treatment.

What is the best 12-episode romance anime?+

Toradora runs longer, but for a tight single-cour watch, Horimiya, My Dress-Up Darling and Bunny Girl Senpai all deliver a complete romance in around 12 to 13 episodes.

What are the best romance anime for beginners?+

Start with Toradora or Horimiya for series, or Your Name for a film. All three are easy to follow, well paced, and do not need any prior anime knowledge.
